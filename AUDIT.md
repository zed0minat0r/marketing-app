# Nigel Audit — TextMarketer Landing Page
**Date:** 2026-04-06
**Perspective:** Small business owner visiting for the first time

## Overall Score: 6.2 / 10

## What Works
- **Value prop is immediately clear.** "Your marketing team lives in your texts" — I get it in under 3 seconds. No jargon.
- **The interactive demo is strong.** Tapping through a mock conversation is far more convincing than a feature list. The "Joe's Coffee" example feels real and relatable for a local business.
- **Pricing is transparent.** Three tiers, no hidden fees, no "contact sales." $49/mo is a reasonable entry point. The "Less than your cheapest employee" headline lands.
- **The anti-SaaS quote section.** Genuinely resonated. This is the exact frustration small business owners have.
- **How It Works is dead simple.** Three steps, all text-based. No learning curve to understand.

## What Needs Work

### Trust & Credibility (biggest gap) — deducted 1.5 pts
- No testimonials, case studies, or social proof of any kind. A first-time visitor has zero reason to believe this works. Even one real quote from a beta user would help.
- No company info, team section, or "about" — feels anonymous.
- No privacy/terms links in the footer. For a product that wants my phone number, that's a red flag.

### Conversion Path — deducted 1.0 pt
- "Start Free Trial" goes nowhere. The final CTA says "Text us your business name" but there's no phone number to text. No form, no input field, no actual way to sign up. This is a dead end.
- Every CTA button scrolls to this same dead-end section. A visitor who's convinced has no way to act.

### Content Gaps — deducted 0.8 pt
- No FAQ section. "What happens after I sign up?" "Can I cancel?" "Do you actually post for me or just draft?" — these are unanswered.
- "100 AI generations/mo" — what counts as a generation? A post? An edit? This is unclear.
- The demo says "5 posts for Joe's Coffee" but the Starter plan says "100 AI generations/mo" — math doesn't obviously connect.

### Design Observations — deducted 0.5 pt
- Dark theme is well-executed and doesn't feel generic. Good use of blue accent without overdoing it.
- The page is clean and fast — no bloat, no unnecessary animations.
- Typography is solid. DM Sans + JetBrains Mono is a good pairing.
- Minor: the step numbers (01, 02, 03) render in a very dark gray that's nearly invisible. Intentional but slightly too subtle.

## Does It Look AI-Generated?
Mostly no. The design breaks away from typical AI-generated patterns — no teal/green palette, no rounded card overload, no generic stock imagery. The copy is punchy and specific. The "Joe's Coffee" demo content grounds it in reality. The monospace accents and dark theme feel more "developer tool" than "AI template," which actually works for this brand.

One flag: the feature card grid with emoji icons is a pattern seen on many AI-generated sites. Consider swapping emojis for simple SVG icons or just removing them.

## Would I Sign Up?
I'd be interested, but I literally cannot sign up. The CTA is a dead end. Fix that and add one piece of social proof, and this jumps to a 7.5+.

## Priority Fixes
1. Add an actual signup mechanism (phone number input, SMS link, anything)
2. Add at least one testimonial or beta user quote
3. Add FAQ section answering basic trust questions
4. Add privacy policy / terms links to footer
5. Swap emoji icons for something less templated
